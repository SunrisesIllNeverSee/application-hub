Smart anti-spam layer. One submission per day per signed-in account is a clean, simple throttle that keeps the platform high-signal and low-noise. It directly protects the "one cold email → distributed widely" hook you described.

**How it strengthens the product**
- **Spam defense:** Prevents mass-dumpers, bots, or desperate founders blasting 50 mediocre pitches daily. Recipients (VCs, operators, etc.) stay happy because their curated inbox doesn't get flooded.
- **Quality incentive:** Forces users to polish their one submission. Better emails = higher votes, better distribution success, more community value.
- **Data integrity:** Each submission becomes a meaningful data point instead of noise. Aggregates (e.g., "This template style got 22% response rate this week") stay trustworthy.
- **Recipient trust:** Opted-in targets receive 1–5 high-quality, matched requests per day max, not dozens of garbage.

**Potential downsides & fixes**

| Issue | Mitigation |
|-------|------------|
| Legit power users blocked (e.g., active fundraiser or sales rep) | Tiered limits: Free = 1/day. Paid/Pro = 3–5/day. Verified users (LinkedIn connect, domain email) = higher. |
| New users frustrated | Onboarding bonus: First week = 2–3 submissions. Or "streak rewards" for consistent good submissions. |
| Evasion (multiple accounts) | Email + phone verification + rate limits by IP/device fingerprint. Light CAPTCHA on submission. |
| Edge cases (big corp needing volume) | Enterprise tier with custom limits + dedicated moderation. |

**Hybrid model that works well for similar communities:**
- Base: 1 submission / 24 hours per account.
- Reputation system: Good votes/responses on past submissions unlock temporary boosts (e.g., +1 extra slot).
- Cooldown resets at midnight UTC or based on user timezone.

This mirrors proven tactics from forums, marketplaces, and outreach tools — daily limits cut spam dramatically while rewarding engaged users.

**Full anti-abuse stack (recommended)**
- One sub/day (your hook).

- Recipient controls: "Mute this category" or "Fewer requests."
- Transparency: Public "submission quality leaderboard" to gamify excellence.

This keeps the flywheel healthy: High-quality pitches → engaged recipients → richer feedback data → better AI suggestions → even stronger submissions.
