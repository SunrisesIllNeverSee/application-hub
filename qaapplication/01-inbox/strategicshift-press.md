**MO§ES™ Defines Constitutional AI Governance as New Category · Publishes Benchmarks Showing 6× to 110× Efficiency Gap Versus Industry Standard**

Across all five measured economic categories (cache hit rate, output-to-input compression, tokens per task, time per task, and cost per line of code), MO§ES ranks #1 against every published combination in the field.

"AA tests ungoverned AI work. MO§ES tests governed AI work," said DJM, the operator of record and managing member of Ello Cello LLC. "The leaderboard tests engines. We test the whole governed vehicle. The deltas aren't because we have a better model. The delta is the operator, and the operator is a governed practice."

The governance framework consists of four primitives: a constitutional document that locks intent and decision rules at session start; a role hierarchy of Primary, Secondary, and Observer agents for parallel attention without conflict; a hash-verified audit chain that carries institutional memory across sessions; and a cache discipline that pre-loads governance context as warm tokens. The patent portfolio (filings 63/877,177, 63/883,018, 19/426,028, 63/991,282, and trademark filing TM 99408355) covers the governance layer above the model, not the model itself.

The empirical receipts are published as open, citable source-of-truth files at mos2es.com/benchmarks, with full raw token extraction (1,123,252,011 total tokens across the window), per-kernel derivation formulas, and cross-comparison against Artificial Analysis's published 13-model Coding Agent Index.

"Pre-AI governance worked because humans have memory, accountability, and self-constraint built in," DJM added. "AI systems have none of those. So we externalized all three. That isn't a workflow. That's a category, and we wrote the framework while shipping the product. The benchmark is the receipt."
